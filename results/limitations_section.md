## Limitations

Several methodological considerations should be acknowledged when interpreting these findings. First, inorganic zinc salts (e.g., zinc chloride, zinc sulfate) are not represented as individual perturbagens in the LINCS L1000 compendium. We therefore employed clioquinol (BRD-K09255212), a well-characterized zinc ionophore, as a pharmacological proxy for zinc-availability perturbation. While clioquinol faithfully recapitulates intracellular zinc elevation and downstream transcriptional responses in multiple cell models, its off-target chelation activity and anti-inflammatory properties introduce a degree of pharmacological ambiguity that cannot be fully disentangled from the zinc-specific signal in the present computational framework.

Second, the LINCS Level 5 consensus signatures used in this study derive predominantly from immortalized cancer and non-follicular cell lines (e.g., MCF7, A549, HT29, PC3). These expression contexts differ substantially from the dermal papilla microenvironment. Although our strongest rescue signal emerged in GSE66663—a dataset generated from immortalized dermal papilla cells—the concordance between LINCS-derived perturbational profiles and native DP biology remains an approximation. The observed signal dilution in bulk scalp tissue datasets (GSE90594, GSE36169) likely reflects both cellular heterogeneity and the mismatch between LINCS source cell types and follicular compartments.

Third, transcriptomic reversal, while a validated computational strategy for drug repositioning, does not guarantee functional rescue at the protein, cellular, or clinical level. The weighted directional convergence scores employed here capture expression-level antagonism between disease and drug signatures but do not model post-transcriptional regulation, protein stability, or pathway crosstalk that may attenuate or amplify the predicted effect in vivo.

Finally, no experimental validation was performed in this study. The statistical robustness of the rescue signal—confirmed through 10,000-iteration permutation null models, leave-10%-out stability analysis, bootstrap resampling, and benchmarking against 200 randomly selected LINCS compounds—supports the specificity of the Li+Zn combination but cannot substitute for functional assays. Future work should prioritize perturbational profiling in primary dermal papilla cultures or hair follicle organoid models to establish whether the transcriptomic convergence observed here translates to measurable improvements in DP signature gene expression, Wnt/β-catenin pathway activation, and ultimately hair follicle neogenesis.
